About Me

"I didn't start from theory — I started from actually doing it."

My path into social media marketing wasn't mapped out — it was discovered in real time. At Aelfric Eden, I was posting content and immediately watching how small changes in hooks or storytelling could shift performance overnight. There was a moment when a video I'd reworked suddenly outperformed everything we'd posted before — and I realized that wasn't luck. It was pattern recognition.

Over four years, I built a workflow where creative instinct generates the idea, and data refines and scales it. I start by asking what feels culturally alive — what's the tension, the timing, the human truth behind a product — then use performance signals to sharpen and commit.

What draws me to clean beauty — and specifically to brands like ILIA — is how the category creates space for storytelling that's both educational and aspirational. Today's audience is informed and intentional; they're not just buying makeup, they're buying into a philosophy of transparency and skin-first beauty.

"The most satisfying moment in any campaign is when it stops feeling like internal work and becomes something visible in the market — where you can see people responding to the brand in real time."
